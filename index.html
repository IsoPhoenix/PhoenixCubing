How to Improve

Advice and tutorials for speedsolving faster can be found below. Find the section corresponding to your current goal below, but make sure you are solid on the skills of previous sections!

Sub-30s:

If you want to average under 30 seconds, you should first learn basic F2L with 2-look OLL and 2-look PLL. When learning, make sure to turn slowly and practice lookahead! A guide to lookahead can be found here. Lookahead is the single most important factor to making your solves faster, so you should always be seeking to improve your lookahead no matter how fast you are.

Sub-20s:

Averaging under 20 seconds takes a fair bit of work, but there are many avenues to improve your solves! First, you should improve your cross and F2L stages. Here are tutorials for advanced cross and advanced F2L. I've also included some of my favorite F2L algorithms above. You should also learn some more advanced algsets. First learn 1-look PLL, then 1-look OLL!

Sub-15s:

Here's where I am in my solves! Improving your lookahead is crucial to averaging under 15 seconds, but learning an algset like COLL should also help. The bottom line is: lookahead, lookahead, lookahead!

Sub-10s:

Averaging under 10 seconds is an incredible milestone that requires a lot of work, but will place you among the top speedcubers in the world. Techniques like X-cross, ZBLL, and color neutrality will aid you immensely. Good luck!
